How It Works

Three steps to smarter
heart health

Thump uses your existing Apple Watch data. No extra hardware, no manual logging, no clinical setup.

1

Wear Your Watch

Your Apple Watch captures over a thousand data points daily — heart rate samples, HRV, recovery, VO2 max, steps, sleep, zone time. Thump reads all of it. Just wear your watch as usual.

2

Get Daily Insights

Our trend engine analyzes your data using robust statistics to detect patterns, anomalies, and regression. You receive a daily assessment with confidence scoring.

3

Your Buddy Checks In

Your buddy reads the signals and checks in at the right moment — a breathing reset when stress spikes, a recovery day when your body needs it. You just respond on your wrist.

Features

Everything your heart
data should tell you

Professional-grade analysis, approachable training. All processing stays on your device.

📊

Robust Trend Engine

Median + MAD statistics resist outlier distortion. Linear regression detects multi-day shifts. Composite anomaly scoring weighs RHR, HRV, recovery, and VO2 max to surface what matters.

🧠

Training Buddy

Your buddy reads your heart data and checks in before you even ask. Stress spiking? It suggests a breathing reset. Trending down? It dials back your plan. The training adapts to you, not the other way around.

⌚

Heart Notifications

Anomaly detected? Your Watch taps your wrist with a clear, actionable notification. Respond with one tap — "felt great" or "not today" — and tomorrow's training adapts. A two-way conversation with your heart.

🔗

Activity Correlations

Pearson correlation analysis reveals which activities most impact your heart metrics — steps vs. RHR, sleep vs. HRV, workouts vs. recovery.

🔔

Smart Alerts

Budget-aware alerting with 8-hour cooldown and 3/day limit prevents fatigue. Alerts trigger only for significant anomaly scores, not minor fluctuations.

🔒

Your Data, Your Devices

All analysis runs on your iPhone and Apple Watch. Anonymous usage analytics help us improve the app, but your health data stays on your devices. You can export it yourself — like sharing a PDF report with your doctor.
