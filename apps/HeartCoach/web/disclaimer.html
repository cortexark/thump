Health Disclaimer

Effective Date: March 1, 2026

This Health Disclaimer applies to the Thump app for iOS and watchOS (the "App"). Please read it carefully before using the App.

1. Wellness Tool, Not a Medical Device

Thump is a wellness and heart training tool designed to help you understand and explore your fitness trends. It is NOT a medical device. The App does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease or health condition.

Thump provides general wellness insights based on data from your Apple Watch and iPhone. These insights are intended to support general wellness goals such as understanding your heart rate trends, improving fitness, tracking recovery, and building healthy habits. They are not intended for clinical use, medical diagnosis, or treatment decisions.

2. FDA General Wellness Classification

Thump is intended to fall within the FDA's General Wellness policy. Per FDA guidance, general wellness products are those that (1) are intended for only general wellness use, and (2) present low risk to users. Thump is intended to promote, track, and encourage a healthy lifestyle by presenting heart health and fitness data in an accessible way. It is not intended for use in the diagnosis of disease or other conditions, or in the cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of disease.

3. Not Intended for Medical Diagnosis

The App's features, including but not limited to heart rate trends, HRV analysis, VO2 max tracking, readiness scores, and training recommendations, are not intended to be used for medical diagnosis. Specifically:

  • Heart rate data displayed in the App is not equivalent to a clinical ECG or Holter monitor
  • HRV measurements are simplified metrics and do not represent clinical-grade autonomic nervous system assessment
  • VO2 max estimates are algorithmic approximations, not laboratory-measured values
  • Sleep analysis is based on motion and heart rate heuristics, not polysomnography
  • Any "scores", "zones", or "readiness" indicators are proprietary calculations for general wellness purposes only

4. Consult a Healthcare Professional

You should always consult with a qualified healthcare professional regarding:

  • Any questions or concerns about a medical condition
  • Starting, changing, or stopping any exercise program or health regimen
  • Any symptoms you experience, including but not limited to chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness, or irregular heartbeat
  • Interpretation of any health metrics or trends shown in the App
  • Any health decisions or changes to medication

Never disregard professional medical advice or delay seeking medical attention because of something you have seen in Thump.

5. Emergency Contacts

If you believe you are experiencing a medical emergency, call 911 (or your local emergency number) immediately.

Thump is not an emergency response system. The App cannot detect medical emergencies, cardiac events, falls, or other dangerous situations. Do not wait for the App to alert you if you feel unwell.

Emergency resources:

Emergency Services: 911 (United States)

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988

Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222

6. Data Accuracy Limitations

Thump processes data from Apple Watch and iPhone sensors. The accuracy of this data depends on many factors outside our control:

  • Sensor limitations: Optical heart rate sensors estimate heart rate using photoplethysmography (PPG), which can be affected by skin perfusion, motion artifacts, and sensor contact quality
  • Device fit: A loose or improperly positioned Apple Watch may produce inaccurate readings
  • Environmental factors: Cold temperatures, sweating, tattoos over the sensor area, and movement can affect measurement accuracy
  • Algorithmic estimation: Metrics such as VO2 max and sleep stages are estimated using algorithms and may not reflect true physiological values
  • Data gaps: Sensor data may contain gaps due to device removal, low battery, connectivity issues, or software limitations
  • Individual variation: Health metrics vary significantly between individuals. What is "normal" for one person may not be normal for another

We make no representation or warranty regarding the accuracy, reliability, or completeness of any data displayed in the App.

7. Apple Watch Sensor Limitations

The Apple Watch is a consumer electronics device, not a medical-grade monitoring system. Important limitations include:

  • The optical heart rate sensor may not work reliably during high-intensity activities with significant wrist movement
  • Heart rate readings may be less accurate for certain skin tones, wrist sizes, or body compositions
  • The Apple Watch does not continuously monitor heart rate; it samples at intervals that may miss transient events
  • Blood oxygen measurements (if used) are not intended for medical use and are not available in all regions
  • Environmental factors such as altitude, temperature, and humidity can affect sensor performance

For detailed information about Apple Watch sensor capabilities and limitations, refer to Apple's official support documentation.

8. HIPAA and Health Data

Thump is not a HIPAA-covered entity. HIPAA (the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) applies to healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses, as well as their business associates. Thump does not fall into any of these categories.

However, we take your health data privacy seriously. As described in our Privacy Policy:

  • All health data is processed and stored locally on your device
  • We never transmit, receive, or store your health data on external servers
  • We do not share health data with third parties
  • You maintain full control over your data at all times

9. Assumption of Risk

By using Thump, you acknowledge that:

  • You understand the App is a general wellness tool and not a medical device
  • You assume all risks associated with using the App's insights for fitness and wellness decisions
  • You will not rely solely on the App for any health-related decisions
  • You understand that exercise and physical activity carry inherent risks
  • You have consulted with a healthcare provider before beginning any new fitness program, if appropriate for your health status
